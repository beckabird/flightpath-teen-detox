## SECTION 1 — BRAND SUMMARY *(paste straight into the deck)*

**Brand idea**
- Flight Path Teen Detox is a medically supervised detox program built only for adolescents (13–17) in Orange County.
- The metaphor is navigation: **recovery as a flight path — a departure, a climb, and a safe place to land.** Not a fall from grace; a charted course with waypoints.

**Primary audiences**
- **Parents (35–55):** scared, exhausted, often calling in the middle of the night at an acute moment. Carrying guilt. Need clarity, credibility, and a team they can trust.
- **Teens (13–17):** resistant, scared, or both. Need to feel like a person, not a patient — no shame, no condescension, no clinical coldness.
- **Referral partners:** therapists, pediatricians, school counselors, clinicians who see teens in distress and have nowhere local to send them. Need a trusted option, a clean referral process, and direct clinical communication.

**Core promises**
- Medically supervised teen detox, ages **13–17**, in Orange County.
- **24/7 admissions and 24/7 medical supervision.**
- **Family-integrated care from day one** — built into the model, not added on.
- **Fast insurance verification; most major plans accepted.**

**Messaging pillars (with page-sourced examples)**
- **Education** — what teen detox is, signs of withdrawal, why adolescent protocols differ. *"Teen withdrawal needs its own protocol. Adolescent physiology is different. So is the approach."*
- **Hope** — milestones and forward motion. *"Every step forward is a waypoint." / "Recovery is not a destination. It is a direction."*
- **Trust** — facility, team, safety, clinical credibility. *"Medically supervised. Genuinely human." / "Built for teens, not adapted from adults."*
- **Action** — insurance verification, 24/7 line, first call. *"The hardest part is the call. We'll take it from there."*

**Tone & voice — the phrases that define it**
- "A safe place to land."
- "Recovery is not a fall from grace. It is a flight path."
- "The hardest part is the call. We'll take it from there."
- "Every step forward is a waypoint."
- "Medically supervised. Genuinely human."
- "Built for teens, not adapted from adults."
- "Every recovery has a starting point. This is a good one."

**Brand pillars (from the Brand Book) — the personality behind the voice**
- **SAFE** — medically supervised, always. Calm is the product.
- **HOPEFUL** — warmth and forward motion, never fear-based.
- **GROUNDED** — real clinical credibility beneath the optimism.
- **HUMAN** — built for families in a hard moment, not algorithms.

## SECTION 2 — THE BRAND SYSTEM (ground-up)

### 2.1 Positioning & story

**One-sentence positioning**
> Flight Path Teen Detox is Orange County's adolescent-only, medically supervised detox program — clinical rigor and genuine warmth for teens 13–17 and the families standing beside them.

**Narrative (2–3 sentences)**
> Orange County treats adults. Almost no one built medically supervised detox for a 15-year-old — so families choose between adult programs that were never designed for their child or waitlists out of county. Flight Path closes that gap with adolescent-specific clinical care, family integration from day one, and a referral partnership clinicians can trust — all carried by a single idea: recovery isn't a fall, it's a flight path with a safe place to land.

**Key differentiators (slide bullets)**
- **Adolescent-only, from day one** — protocols, staff, and environment built for 13–17, not shrunk down from adult programs.
- **Medically supervised, 24/7** — a safety-first detox standard, not a wilderness camp or a boot camp.
- **Family-integrated by design** — parents are oriented and involved from the first call, not excluded until discharge.
- **Referral-ready** — direct clinical communication and a clean, HIPAA-aware intake built for therapists, pediatricians, and school counselors.
- **A brand teens aren't ashamed of** — premium and calm enough to reassure a parent, human enough not to alienate a resistant teen, credible enough to earn a clinical referral.

**Voice checklist (for the investor "consistency" slide)**

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Lead with the result, then the care. | Open with fear or worst-case framing. |
| Use person-first language: "a teen experiencing substance use." | Say "addict," "junkie," or label the teen. |
| Keep CTAs concrete and low-pressure ("Talk to our team"). | Use hard-sell or form-speak ("Submit," "Apply now"). |
| Stay HIPAA-aware; never request or imply storing health info in marketing. | Collect or hint at clinical detail in public channels. |
| Speak to the parent and the teen at once. | Talk down to teens or over the heads of parents. |
| Be honest that recovery is work. | Overstate outcomes or promise a "cure." |
| Run any clinical claim past licensed review. | Publish clinical claims unreviewed. |

### 2.3 Visual identity *(direction confirmed by Brand Book v1)*

**Moodboard descriptor (6–8 words)**
> Calm aviation, premium clinical, warm runway-at-dawn navy.

**Color system — "Runway at Dawn"** *(hexes from the Brand Book)*
- **Primary:** Flight Navy `#0A2550` · Midnight `#07172F` (dark backgrounds) · Navy Surface `#102A52` (cards/panels)
- **Accent / CTA:** Signal Orange `#EF5B0E` · Sunrise `#F26B22` (orange on dark) · Ember `#C8410F` (text-safe orange for small type)
- **Neutrals:** Stone `#EEE9DF` (warm neutral) · Cloud `#FCFBF8` (near-white)
- **Rules:** flat color fields, never decorative gradients. On cream, set body in Flight Navy and reserve Ember for small orange text. On navy, use Cloud for text and the brighter Sunrise for accents and links. Signal Orange marks the runway and the climb — use it for action, never for body copy.

**Typography system**
| Use | Typeface | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Display / headlines / eyebrows | **Oswald** | UPPERCASE, letterspaced, weights 300–700; tightest free match to the wordmark. |
| "TEEN DETOX" kicker / tight labels | **Barlow Semi Condensed**, SemiBold | The tagline-style lockup feel. Reserved — never body. |
| Body / UI / forms | **Barlow** | Sentence case, warm, highly legible. |
- **Mapping:** Hero & section titles → Oswald UPPERCASE · Kickers/labels → Barlow Semi Condensed SemiBold · Paragraphs, captions, forms → Barlow.

**Logo system** *(as drawn in the Brand Book)*
- **Primary:** horizontal lockup — prop-plane roundel + "FLIGHT PATH / TEEN DETOX." Use wherever width allows (site header, letterhead, email signature).
- **Variants:** stacked lockup · emblem (roundel only) · wordmark · reversed (on Flight Navy / brand orange, use the Cloud artwork).
- **Clear space** = the height of the "T" in TEEN DETOX on all sides. **Minimums:** horizontal lockup 160px wide; emblem 32px. Below 32px, drop the runway detail.

**Key motifs & where they show up**
- **Roundel + prop plane** — emblem on patches, caps, favicons, social avatar.
- **The runway / horizon line** — a thin Signal-Orange rule used as section divider and the plane's takeoff streak; recurs on letterhead, banners, postcards.
- **Waypoints** — small dots/ticks marking milestones; natural fit for the three-phase model, progress states, and "every step forward is a waypoint."
- **Departure → climb → land** — the narrative arc that orders web sections, the deck, and the program model.
- **Print vs web:** print favors the emblem + a single runway rule on Stone/Cloud; web favors full-bleed navy with the horizon line drawing in on scroll.

### 2.4 Program model *(proposed architecture — identity-only in the Brand Book; included as the clinical story)*
- **Phase 01 · Ground School** — medically supervised stabilization (24/7 supervision, adolescent withdrawal protocols, family orientation, psychiatric evaluation).
- **Phase 02 · The Climb** — residential treatment (individual + group therapy, family systems work, academic continuity, dual-diagnosis assessment).
- **Phase 03 · Cruise Altitude** — aftercare & reintegration (step-down outpatient, continuing-care plan, school reintegration, alumni connection).
> One continuous arc, not three separate programs — maps to real clinical sequencing: stabilize, treat, reintegrate.

## SECTION 3 — SCOPE & PRICING *(US boutique-agency ballparks — adjustable placeholders, not quotes)*

> All ranges are **adjustable placeholders** for a small-brand healthcare context. They assume Karle Kreatives leads design and partners with a developer/low-code stack. They exclude media spend, photography/illustration licensing, printing, and ongoing retainer unless noted.

### Phase 1 — Strategy & Identity (Brand Ground-Up)
**Deliverables:** brand discovery & competitive audit · positioning statement + narrative · visual direction (moodboard, color, type, motif definitions) · logo + lockups + usage basics · core messaging framework (parents, teens, clinicians) · **outputs:** brand one-pager + simple brand guide (PDF).
**Ballpark:** **$12,000–$28,000** (≈ 80–160 hrs). *Much of this is already executed in Brand Book v1 — position as "completed / de-risked" to investors; remaining spend is finalization and the messaging framework.*

### Phase 2 — Web & UX (Core site)
**Deliverables:** site architecture (Home, Parents, Clinicians/Referrals, Insurance/Admissions, FAQ, About, Contact) · admissions UX flows (call, insurance verification, intake) · hi-fi homepage + 2 key subpages · build on a CMS / low-code stack.
**Assumptions:** CMS = Webflow or WordPress; **no EMR integration in v1**; insurance verification is a lightweight, HIPAA-aware lead form (no PHI stored in marketing site); analytics + basic SEO included.
- **Design:** $14,000–$30,000
- **Build/implementation:** $10,000–$24,000
- **Phase 2 band:** **$24,000–$54,000**

### Phase 3 — Marketing Collateral & Launch Kit (v1 = templates, not infinite versions)

**A. Social media system** — IG grid + stories templates, LinkedIn tiles, basic Reels/TikTok framing; refined sample posts; content pillars (**Education 35% · Hope 25% · Trust 20% · Action 20%**) as a reusable monthly calendar.
- *Growth/referral case:* organic proof for parents searching at 2am and a credible feed clinicians can vet before referring.
- *Ballpark:* **$6,000–$14,000** (template system + 1 starter month).

**B. Print & office collateral** — letterhead + digital letter template · business cards · intake/welcome packet cover + interior page system · email signature system.
- *Growth/referral case:* a referral letter and welcome packet are the physical trust artifacts that move a clinician from "aware" to "refers regularly."
- *Ballpark:* **$5,000–$12,000.**

**C. Conference & B2B** — 10–12 slide investor/clinician deck template · 1–2 tabletop/pop-up banner concepts · clinician/school one-sheet handout.
- *Growth/referral case:* the field kit that wins clinician relationships and B2B referral contracts at conferences and lunch-and-learns.
- *Ballpark:* **$6,000–$13,000.**

**D. Merch / environment** — staff apparel concepts (scrub patch, crewneck, hoodie, hat) · one family-facing item (mug/notebook/tote) aligned to "waypoints" / "safe place to land."
- *Growth/referral case:* staff apparel signals a real, cohesive program on tour days; the family item is a quiet retention/word-of-mouth touch.
- *Ballpark:* **$3,500–$8,000** (design/concepts; production billed at cost).
